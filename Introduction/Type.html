Type of Coffee Beans

Arabica, the first coffee variety to be discovered, is now the dominant force in the global consumer market, accounting for more than 70% of the total. Its uniqueness lies in its excellent acidity, as well as its fresh and pleasant floral aroma and intoxicating taste, bringing coffee lovers a rich and diverse flavor experience.

Robusta coffee originates from the Congo region of Africa and accounts for about 20 to 25 percent of global coffee cultivation. Although its flavor is not as excellent as other varieties and its caffeine content is relatively high, it is cheap to grow and rich in oils, so it is often used in blends or instant coffee.

Liberica coffee has a small market share, only about 2% of global consumption, and is not used on a daily basis due to its greater scarcity and higher price. With a larger tree and fruit than other varieties, Liberica coffee has a teardrop-like shape and a flavor similar to Robusta. Its popularity stems from its high caffeine content as well as its floral aroma and sharp, dry taste.
